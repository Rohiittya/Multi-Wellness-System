Gluten-Free Indian Weight-Loss Recipes

1. Palak Paneer

Ingredients:

  • 200g paneer, cubed
  • 4 cups spinach leaves, washed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 tomato, pureed
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste

Procedure:

  1. Blanch the spinach leaves in boiling water for 2 minutes, then blend them into a smooth puree.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and sauté until they crackle.
  3. Add chopped onion, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
  5. Add the spinach puree and mix well. Season with salt and garam masala.
  6. Add the paneer cubes and simmer for 5 minutes. Serve hot.

2. Moong Dal Chilla

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup moong dal, soaked overnight
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for cooking

Procedure:

  1. Grind the soaked moong dal with green chili and ginger into a smooth batter.
  2. Add chopped onion, coriander leaves, and salt to the batter. Mix well.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan and grease it with a little oil.
  4. Pour a ladleful of batter and spread it into a thin circle. Cook until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Serve hot with chutney.

3. Masala Oats

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup oats
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste

Procedure:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them crackle.
  2. Add chopped onion, green chili, and grated carrot. Sauté until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add chopped tomato and peas. Cook until the tomato is soft.
  4. Add oats, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add 2 cups of water and cook until the oats are done. Serve hot.

4. Baingan Bharta

Ingredients:

  • 1 large eggplant
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Procedure:

  1. Roast the eggplant on an open flame until the skin is charred. Let it cool and peel off the skin. Mash the pulp.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them crackle.
  3. Add chopped onion, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.
  5. Add the mashed eggplant and mix well. Cook for 10 minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

5. Karela Sabzi

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium-sized bitter gourds (karela)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp mango powder (amchur)

Procedure:

  1. Wash the bitter gourds, cut them into thin slices, and remove the seeds.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  4. Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add sliced bitter gourds and cook on low heat until they are tender, stirring occasionally.
  6. Sprinkle mango powder (amchur) on top, mix well, and cook for another 2 minutes. Serve hot.

6. Rajma Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked kidney beans (rajma)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 cucumber, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Procedure:

  1. In a large bowl, combine cooked kidney beans, chopped onion, tomato, cucumber, and green chili.
  2. Add chopped coriander leaves, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Serve the salad chilled or at room temperature.

7. Tandoori Cauliflower

Ingredients:

  • 1 large cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp tandoori masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Procedure:

  1. In a bowl, combine Greek yogurt, tandoori masala, turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, salt, and lemon juice.
  2. Add cauliflower florets to the bowl and coat them evenly with the marinade. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F).
  4. Place the marinated cauliflower florets on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the cauliflower is tender and slightly charred.
  6. Serve hot with a squeeze of lemon and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

8. Quinoa Pulao

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste

Procedure:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan, add cumin seeds and let them crackle.
  2. Add chopped onion, carrot, and bell pepper. Sauté until vegetables are tender.
  3. Add rinsed quinoa, garam masala, salt, and 2 cups of water. Bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer until quinoa is cooked and water is absorbed. Fluff with a fork and serve hot.

9. Vegetable Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Procedure:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pot, add chopped onion and garlic, sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add carrot, zucchini, and bell pepper, sauté for 5 minutes.
  3. Add diced tomatoes and vegetable broth, bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

10. Chana Masala

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Procedure:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan, add cumin seeds and let them crackle.
  2. Add chopped onion, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add tomato puree, turmeric powder, chili powder, and salt. Cook until oil separates.
  4. Add cooked chickpeas and garam masala. Simmer for 10 minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.

11. Beetroot Raita

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 medium beetroot, grated
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder

Procedure:

  1. In a bowl, whisk yogurt until smooth.
  2. Add grated beetroot, chopped green chili, coriander leaves, salt, and cumin powder. Mix well.
  3. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving.

12. Ragi Dosa

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ragi flour
  • 1/2 cup rice flour
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Water to make batter
  • Oil for cooking

Procedure:

  1. In a bowl, mix ragi flour, rice flour, yogurt, chopped green chili, coriander leaves, and salt.
  2. Add water to make a thin batter. Mix well and let it rest for 15 minutes.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan and grease it with a little oil.
  4. Pour a ladleful of batter and spread it into a thin circle. Cook until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Serve hot with chutney.
